Phishing Education

Learn how to identify and protect yourself from phishing attempts

Warning Signs

  • Suspicious URLs

    Watch for misspelled domain names, unusual TLDs, or URLs with random characters.

  • Urgent Requests

    Be wary of messages creating false urgency to make you act without thinking.

  • Requests for Personal Information

    Legitimate organizations won't ask for sensitive information via email or messages.

Safety Tips

  • Verify the URL

    Always check the full URL in your browser's address bar before entering any information.

  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication

    Enable 2FA on all your important accounts for an extra layer of security.

  • Go Directly to Websites

    Instead of clicking links, type the website address directly in your browser.

Common Phishing Examples

Learn to recognize these common phishing tactics

Fake Login Pages

Scammers create fake login pages that look identical to legitimate websites like banks, social media, or email providers.

Example: "faceb00k-login.com" instead of "facebook.com"

Account Verification Scams

Emails claiming your account will be suspended unless you "verify" your information immediately.

Example: "Your PayPal account has been limited. Verify now."

Package Delivery Notifications

Fake delivery notifications claiming you need to pay a fee or provide information to receive a package.

Example: "Your package is held at customs. Pay fee to release."
